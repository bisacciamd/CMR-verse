The CMR-verse

A quantitative appraisal of CMR publications

This document reports on a systematic search which explores the landscape of cardiac magnetic resonance (CMR)-related publications in the last years (2017-2024).

Journals

  • Journal search on Scopus was restricted to the top 10% journals in cardiology and radiology on the Scopus database
  • A total of 31 journals in the Cardiology and Cardiovascular Medicine category, and 24 journals in the Radiology, Nuclear Medicine and Imaging category were identified as the top 10% journals in their respective category (n = 55 total journals).
    • of these, 20 journals had articles related to CMR.

Papers

  • A systematic search was conducted for all CMR-related studies indexed on PubMed as of 2024-08-11 with a date of publication between 2017 to 2024.
  • Between 2017 and 2024, a total of 3144 CMR-related papers were published. Of these, 332 (%) were published in top 10% journals by a total of 304 unique first authors and 257 unique last authors.

Authors

  • Author search on Scopus was limited to first and last authors with > 1 CMR-related publications.

Last authors by specialty

  • Specialty was inferred through combination of automated and manual searches based on Google search results.
  • Cardiology as the main specialty was inferred if the author’s CV or relevant online statements (e.g. LinkedIn) referred to a clinical fellowship or professorship in cardiology; if any statements were retrieved that the author is an expert in clinical electrophysiology, interventional cardiology, and/or heart failure; if the author’s ResearchGate profile listed clinical cardiology among the author’s interests.
  • Radiology as the main specialty was inferred if the author’s CV or relevant online statements (e.g. LinkedIn) referred to a fellowship or professorship in radiology; if the author was affiliated to a department of radiology and published on imaging subjects different than cardiovascular imaging; if the author’s ResearchGate profile listed radiology as the author’s interest.
  • a total of 146 authors specializing in Cardiology and 67 authors specializing in Radiology were identified worldwide.
  • Specialty was not assigned to 0 due to paucity of data.
